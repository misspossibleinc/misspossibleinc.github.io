Like many startup stories, this one begins in a dorm.

But not just any dorm—a living learning community at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. A place where innovators, entrepreneurs, and doers were all under one roof—including Janna and Supriya.

The girls hit it off instantly. Both female engineers. Both big dreamers. Both surrounded by males in their classes—how frustrating. Where were all the girls?

Immersing themselves in their entrepreneurial and female engineering communities, something quickly became clear: Janna and Supriya were passionate about inspiring girls. And so they did.

They spent hundreds of hours volunteering in schools, promoting engineering through engaging activities. They were testing the waters, and trying to define the impact they wanted to make.

An idea clicked: they realized that, above all, they wanted to empower girls to do something BIG—whatever that may be.

In pursuit of this mission, Miss Possible was born: dolls made a familiar medium for sharing their messages with girls and an online community helped them develop girls’ skills.

The message spread, and the team grew. We are growing, learning, and working together to inspire girls to dream big and give them the tools to achieve.
