Meet Janna & Supriya: Empowering Girls One Doll At a Time

At U lala, we’re all about inspiring and empowering girls. So, when we got the chance to sit down with the newest startup coming out of our very own University of Illinois, we couldn’t contain our excitement. Introducing Miss Possible and the brilliant minds behind the company. We are in la-la-love and you will be, too!

Meet the intelligent and inspirational co-founders, Janna Eaves and Supriya Hobbs. Both Engineering students at the University of Illinois, the dynamic duo are on a mission to empower girls to make a global impact.

How are they doing this?

Miss Possible is an educational toy start-up developing a collection of dolls and online community.

But these are no ordinary dolls. Miss Possible dolls are 10-year-old versions of historical and modern influential women. The dolls model women from all career backgrounds: the writer, doctor, artist, activist and engineer – the list goes on.

Miss Possible: Saving Girls From Limitation One Doll At A Time

Janna Eaves and Supriya Hobbs, engineering students at University of Illinois, are on a mission to empower girls to do something BIG – whatever that may be.

In pursuit of this mission, the two friends pooled their passion and big dreams to create Miss Possible, an interactive online community and collection of dolls that represent real women who changed the world, to inspire girls to do the same.

The dolls, which are currently in development, will portray historical female trailblazers such as Marie Curie, the Nobel Prize-winning physicist and chemist, as 10-year old girls. The dolls, targeting girls 6-10, will feature reasonable, child-like proportions, modest wardrobe and coordinating accessories, i.e. Marie Curie will come with a science kit.

Firm wants to educate, inspire with dolls of historical figures

CHAMPAIGN — Janna Eaves and Supriya Hobbs want to encourage girls to consider careers in science and engineering — and they think they can do it with dolls.

The two University of Illinois students have started a company, Miss Possible, that aims to produce dolls based on historical figures, such as Marie Curie, the Nobel Prize-winning physicist and chemist who discovered the elements radium and polonium.

The dolls, which would portray the historical figures as 10-year-old children, would be more than just playthings.
