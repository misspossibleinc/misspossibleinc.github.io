Meet Our Team

Cofounder, UIUC '14, Chemical Engineering

I grew up surrounded by math and science—playtime meant immersing myself with molecular modeling kits. It seemed only natural I would study Chemical Engineering with the goal to make an impact on the world. After volunteering and promoting STEM in schools, I found my cause: inspiring girls to do something different. Something unique. I found my outlet: Miss Possible. I believe girls will become the majority of all world-changers—but girls must too. I want every girl to see that changing the world is a real possibility.

Cofounder, UIUC '15, Materials Science and Engineering

For my whole life, I struggled with stereotypes and overcoming them. It wasn't until I started practicing engineering outreach that I realized how powerful society’s expectations could become. I soon asked myself, "Why don't more girls pursue engineering?" And the answer that stood out was this: "You can't be what you can't see.” I am frustrated with how women are portrayed in media, and how much it limits the options we see for ourselves. I co-founded Miss Possible because I want to open doors for the girls who come with me and come after me. I believe that anyone can change the world, and they must believe this too.

  • Connect with Kelly:

Web Developer, UIUC '17, Computer Science

I always loved toys—just not dolls. My dolls were tinker toys, my doll accessories were stethoscopes, and my doll houses were science kits. Dolls didn’t look like me, like what I liked, and do what I did. Beauty did not include intelligence. Then came college when I studied computer science. I was told I couldn’t keep up … should pursue something different. And then I realized. I was making a difference; I became a role model. We confine ourselves; we must learn that anything is possible. Intelligence is beauty.
